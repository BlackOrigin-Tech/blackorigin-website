State of Build

Year One  ·  March 2026  ·  The Founder

Every year, the founder of BlackOrigin publishes an honest account of what the company built, what was hard, and what is next. This is not a press release. It is a record.

01

Where we started

BlackOrigin started with a question, not a business plan. Why is it so hard for people to access technology that was actually built for them? Not for institutions. Not for enterprises. For them, specifically.

We incorporated in 2026 in Chicago. No office. No outside funding. Just a clear problem and a strong belief that software can carry the same care and craft as any physical work worth doing.

02

Campus Music goes live

Campus Music is BlackOrigin's first product. It is a platform built for the music community on college campuses. Campuses are where music careers begin, where artists find their first audiences, and where student promoters learn to run events on no budget. The tools available to them were built for the industry, not for where the industry starts. Campus Music closes that gap.

In Year One, Campus Music moved from concept to live product. We built the core experience, put it in front of real users, and listened. Not to validate what we already believed. To understand what we missed. There was a lot we missed. That is the only honest way to build.

"We are not trying to build many things quickly. We are trying to build one thing so well that it earns the right to exist for a long time."

The Founder, 2026
03

What was hard

Doing one thing at a time is harder than it sounds. There is always a reason to expand scope or chase a parallel opportunity. The discipline to hold focus on a single product and give it everything before moving on has to be chosen again every week.

Building for a community requires more patience than building for a market. A market tells you what it will pay for. A community tells you what it needs. Those are not always the same thing. Campus Music taught us to listen to the slower, harder signal even when it is more difficult to act on.

04

What is next

Campus Music continues. Year Two is about depth, not scale. More features that matter to the people already using it, not features designed to attract people who have not arrived yet.

OriginOS is in development. It is BlackOrigin's second product and will say something different about what this company is capable of building. We are not ready to share more than that yet. When we are, this page will be the first place we say it.

We are looking for investors who think in years, not quarters. If that is you, the contact page is open.

We publish this account because transparency is part of how we build. The next edition arrives in 2027.
