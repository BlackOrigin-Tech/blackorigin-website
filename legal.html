This page covers both the legal disclosures for BlackOrigin, Inc. as a corporate entity and the privacy practices that govern how we handle information when you interact with our website and products. We've written this to be clear and direct. If you have questions, reach us at info@blackorigin.org.

‍

1. About This Page

Summary: This page tells you who BlackOrigin is, how we're structured legally, and how we treat data from people who use our products or visit our website.

BlackOrigin, Inc. operates both as a company with legal obligations to its stakeholders and as a technology product company with obligations to its users. This page brings both of those commitments together in one place. The corporate disclosures reflect our formal legal structure as a Delaware corporation. The privacy practices apply to anyone who uses our website at blackorigin.org or our product, Campus Music, at campusmusic.app.

‍

2. Company Information

Summary: BlackOrigin, Inc. is a Delaware C-Corporation founded in March 2026, headquartered in Chicago, Illinois.

BlackOrigin, Inc. was incorporated in the State of Delaware on March 2, 2026 as a C-Corporation. The company's registered agent in Delaware is Legalinc Corporate Services Inc., 131 Continental Dr, Suite 305, Newark, DE 19713. The principal executive office of the company is located in Chicago, Illinois.

BlackOrigin was founded by Kofi Gilbert, who serves as Chief Executive Officer, President, and Secretary of the corporation. The Board of Directors was constituted by Unanimous Written Consent on March 4, 2026. The company's fiscal year ends on December 31 of each year.

BlackOrigin is authorized to issue up to 10,000,000 shares of Common Stock at a par value of $0.00001 per share. The company is governed by its Certificate of Incorporation and Bylaws, both executed under the Delaware General Corporation Law (DGCL).

‍

3. Our Products & Services

Summary: BlackOrigin builds focused technology products. Campus Music is live. OriginOS is in active development.

BlackOrigin follows a disciplined model: we build and scale one product at a time with long-term intent. Our current portfolio includes:

  • Campus Music — A live music platform built for college campuses, connecting artists and audiences in university communities. Available at campusmusic.app.
  • OriginOS — An operating system product currently in development. No public release date has been announced.

This website (blackorigin.org) is our corporate home and is separate from the individual product experiences. Each product may maintain its own terms and privacy documentation as it matures.

‍

4. Information We Collect

Summary: We collect information you provide to us directly and some information automatically when you use our website or products.

When you interact with our website or Campus Music, we may collect:

  • Contact information you submit through forms (such as name, email address, and any message content)
  • Technical data collected automatically, including IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited, and time spent on pages
  • Account information if you create an account on Campus Music, including your name, email, and university affiliation
  • Usage data such as features accessed, content interacted with, and session activity within our products

We do not knowingly collect personal data from individuals under the age of 13. If we learn we have collected such data without appropriate consent, we will delete it promptly.

‍

5. How We Use Your Information

Summary: We use information to operate our products, communicate with you, and improve what we build — never to sell to third parties.

Information we collect is used to:

  • Deliver and maintain the core functionality of Campus Music and this website
  • Respond to inquiries, investor interest, or job applications submitted through our site
  • Analyze usage patterns to improve product performance and user experience
  • Ensure the security and integrity of our systems and prevent misuse
  • Comply with applicable legal obligations

We will not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to any third party for their marketing purposes.

‍

6. Data Sharing & Third Parties

Summary: We only share information when necessary to operate our services, and we hold third-party partners to strict standards.

We may share information with trusted service providers who help us operate our products and website — such as hosting infrastructure, analytics platforms, and communication tools. These providers act under our instruction and may not use your data for their own purposes.

We may also disclose information if required to do so by law, or if we believe in good faith that such disclosure is necessary to comply with a legal obligation, protect our rights, or respond to a government request. In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, user information may be transferred as part of that transaction, with appropriate notice provided.

We do not sell personal information.

‍

7. Intellectual Property

Summary: All content, software, and technology created by BlackOrigin belongs to the company. We take IP protection seriously.

All intellectual property associated with BlackOrigin's products and services — including the Campus Music platform, OriginOS, our brand, design systems, software code, and proprietary methodologies — is owned exclusively by BlackOrigin, Inc. All employees and contractors who contribute to our work sign a Confidential Information and Inventions Assignment Agreement (CIIAA) that assigns all work product and inventions to the company.

Nothing on this website or in our products grants you any license to BlackOrigin's intellectual property unless explicitly stated in a separate written agreement. The BlackOrigin name, logo, and product names are trademarks of BlackOrigin, Inc.

If you believe any content on our website or products infringes on your intellectual property rights, please contact us at info@blackorigin.org.

‍

8. Your Rights

Summary: You have the right to access, correct, or request deletion of your personal data. We'll respond promptly.

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to:

  • Request access to the personal information we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Request deletion of your personal data, subject to our legal obligations
  • Object to or restrict certain types of processing
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at info@blackorigin.org. We will acknowledge and respond to requests within a reasonable timeframe and in accordance with applicable law.

‍

9. Data Retention & Security

Summary: We keep data only as long as needed and apply security practices appropriate for an early-stage technology company.

We retain personal information for as long as is necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this page, or as required by applicable law. When data is no longer needed, we delete or anonymize it. Retention periods vary depending on the nature of the data and the context in which it was collected.

We implement technical and organizational measures to protect information against unauthorized access, loss, alteration, or disclosure. This includes access controls, encrypted communications, and regular reviews of our practices. While no system can be entirely secure, we are committed to treating your information with care and maintaining security practices that are appropriate for the sensitivity of the data we hold.
